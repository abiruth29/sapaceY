Earth Info Center

Explore Our changing Planet

One Government working for one planet




The Earth Information Center (EIC) serves as a centralized hub for accessing comprehensive data, insights, and resources related to Earth observation and environmental monitoring. Through Earth.gov, users can access a wealth of information sourced from various federal government agencies, research institutions, and interagency cooperative efforts, facilitating a coordinated approach to understanding and addressing environmental challenges. One of the key initiatives facilitated by Earth.gov is the U.S. Greenhouse Gas Center (USGGC), which focuses on monitoring and analyzing greenhouse gas emissions and their impact on climate change. The USGGC leverages data from satellites, ground-based monitoring stations, and other sources to track the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, assess trends over time, and identify sources of emissions. The data and insights provided by the EIC and USGGC are invaluable for informing decision-making and policy development at the federal, state, and local levels.


Here are some specific ways in which these data are being used to prepare for climate change and address environmental challenges:

1. Climate Monitoring and Modeling: The EIC collects and disseminates data on key climate indicators such as temperature, precipitation, sea level rise, and ice melt. These data are used to monitor changes in Earth's climate system, validate climate models, and project future climate scenarios, helping policymakers and stakeholders understand the potential impacts of climate change on various sectors.

2. Vulnerability Assessments: Federal agencies and local governments use Earth observation data to conduct vulnerability assessments and identify regions and communities at risk from climate change-related hazards such as extreme weather events, sea level rise, and drought. These assessments inform strategies for building resilience and adapting to changing environmental conditions.

3. Mitigation Strategies: The USGGC provides data and analysis to support efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and mitigate climate change. This includes tracking emissions from industrial sources, transportation, agriculture, and land use changes, as well as assessing the effectiveness of emission reduction policies and initiatives.

4. Natural Resource Management: Earth observation data are used to monitor the health and integrity of natural ecosystems, including forests, wetlands, and coastal areas. This information is used to inform land management practices, conservation efforts, and restoration projects aimed at preserving biodiversity and ecosystem services.

5. Emergency Response and Disaster Management: Earth observation satellites play a critical role in monitoring and responding to natural disasters such as hurricanes, wildfires, floods, and earthquakes. Real-time data on weather patterns, vegetation dynamics, and terrain conditions help emergency responders assess the extent of damage, prioritize resources, and coordinate disaster relief efforts.

6. Public Awareness and Education: Earth.gov serves as a valuable resource for raising public awareness about climate change and environmental issues. Through educational outreach, interactive tools, and multimedia resources, the EIC engages citizens, educators, and stakeholders in understanding the science of climate change and the importance of taking action to protect the planet.


Agriculture

Learn how Earth data are being used to help farmers manage their crops in a changing climate.

Air Quality

The quality of the air you breathe every day has a big impact on your health. Air quality monitors on the ground and in space help us see where air pollution is improving and where there is more work to be done.

Biodiversity

Our Earth is home to millions of species on land and in water. Explore data from across the federal government on biological diversity, how and why it is changing and its effects on and interactions with the Earth system.

Disasters

Learn how satellites and ground surveys are providing actionable data to help responders prepare for disasters, to map widespread damage, to guide recovery and build more resilient communities.

Sustainable Energy

Energy impacts everything we do, from heating and cooling homes and buildings, to providing development opportunities and improving quality of life. Learn how Earth data are used to better understand where energy resources exist and how they are impacted by physical environmental conditions, weather and climate.

Greenhouse Gases

Greenhouse gases (GHGs) in the Earth's atmosphere trap heat and slow heat loss into space. Increases in the concentrations of greenhouse gases, from burning fossil fuels, has caused cascading changes to many of Earth's vital life-supporting systems. Learn how federal data are being used to shed light on the sources and hotspots of GHG emissions and to understand the associated impacts on the climate.

Sea Level Rise

Global sea levels are rising at rates that are unprecedented over the past 2,500 years. Explore how sea level rise has changed over time and learn how Earth data are helping communities plan for changing sea levels.

Wildfires

Wildfires near homes can be deadly when they are out of control, but wildfires are also key to keeping ecosystems healthy and thriving. Learn about how wildfires have intensified with a changing climate across the world and how Earth data are being used to identify, track and monitor wildfires and their associated risks.

Water Resources

Water is one of Earth's most vital resources, but in many regions, water supplies are increasingly scarce. Learn how Earth data are improving monitoring and forecasting of water use, availability and water extremes.
